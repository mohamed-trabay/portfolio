About Me

My journey into tech wasn't a straight line—it started with curiosity and turned into passion. When I first joined the Faculty of Computer and Information Science at Mansoura University, I wasn't sure which path in technology was right for me. Between the lectures, labs, and endless new concepts, it sometimes felt overwhelming. But little by little, I discovered something that truly clicked with me: mobile development.

In my second year, I was introduced to Flutter, and everything changed. The idea of building cross-platform apps with a single codebase felt like magic. I started experimenting, creating small apps, and learning how to bring ideas to life. What began as practice soon became a passion—I loved seeing real users interact with something I built.

Through internships and projects, I developed my skills in Flutter, Dart, and state management (Bloc/Cubit, MVVM), while also diving into Firebase and REST APIs to build complete, scalable solutions. At NTI, I worked on apps that improved performance and created real-world impact. I've built multiple projects ranging from e-commerce platforms to real-time chat applications—including apps now live on both Google Play and App Store.

One of my proudest moments was my graduation project, where our team built a smart home IoT system designed to reduce energy consumption through automation. It showed me the power of tech not just to solve problems, but to create measurable real-world impact.

Today, I'm a Flutter Developer focused on crafting responsive, high-performance mobile apps. My toolkit includes Firebase, REST APIs, payment integration (Stripe/Paymob), and clean architecture, but more importantly, I bring persistence, problem-solving, and a drive to keep improving.

I also love sharing knowledge—whether it's through GitHub projects or helping others learn Flutter basics—because I believe tech grows stronger when we grow together.

What once started as uncertainty has become my passion and career path, and I'm excited to keep building apps that make a difference.
